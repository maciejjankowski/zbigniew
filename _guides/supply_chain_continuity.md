## Supply Chain Analytical Framework

### 1. Network Topology Analysis

Map the supply chain as an intelligence network:

| Element | Supply Chain Equivalent | What to Assess |
|---------|------------------------|----------------|
| Nodes | Suppliers, manufacturers, distributors | Criticality score (0-1), replaceability time |
| Edges | Transport routes, contracts, relationships | Capacity, latency, cost, vulnerability |
| Chokepoints | Single-source suppliers, ports, regulatory gates | Bypass options, failure probability |
| Redundancy | Alternative suppliers, safety stock, dual-sourcing | Coverage ratio, activation time |
| Dark nodes | Sub-tier suppliers (Tier 2-4) | Visibility score, risk propagation |

**Key question:** "What is the minimum viable network? Remove nodes one by one - where does it break?"

### 2. Shadow Sensing for Supply Chains

Detect disruptions by monitoring absence:

| Signal Type | What Stopped | Probable Cause |
|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| Communication | Supplier response time increased 2x+ | Internal crisis, capacity issues |
| Commercial | Competitor suddenly dual-sourcing | They know something about shared supplier |
| Financial | Supplier's insurance premiums spiked | Underwriter sees risk you don't |
| Logistics | Port dwell times increasing gradually | Infrastructure stress, labor issues |
| Regulatory | Compliance certificates delayed | Government policy shift incoming |
| Personnel | Key account manager changed 3x in 6 months | Organizational instability |
| Market | Raw material futures diverging from spot | Anticipated shortage |

**ZBIGNIEW principle:** "The bird stopped singing six weeks ago. Your Tier 2 supplier stopped responding to RFQs last month. Same signal."

### 4. GEOFIZYKA: Supply Chain Physics

Model supply chains using physics analogues:

**Fluid Dynamics:**
- Material flow = fluid flow. Chokepoints create pressure differentials.
- Just-in-time = laminar flow (efficient but fragile). Disruption = turbulence.
- Phase transition: JIT -> JIC (just-in-case) = liquid -> solid. Energy cost is real.
- Backpressure: when downstream stops, upstream still produces. Inventory accumulates. Bullwhip effect.

**Network Resilience:**
- Supply network = electrical circuit. Single-source = series connection (one fails, all fail). Dual-source = parallel (redundancy).
- Critical path analysis: which node failure causes maximum cascade?
- Minimum spanning tree: what's the leanest network that still functions?

**Oscillation:**
- Bullwhip effect = standing wave in supply chain. Amplifies upstream.
- Inventory cycles = damped oscillation. Safety stock = damping coefficient.
- Demand signal distortion = noise in transmission line. Closer to source = cleaner signal.

### 6. Change Management Intelligence

When supply chain changes are planned (new supplier, reshoring, material substitution):

**Pre-mortem questions:**
- "It's 12 months after the switch. It failed. Why?"
- "Which assumption about the new supplier have we not verified?"
- "What does the incumbent supplier do when they find out?"
- "Who inside our organization benefits from the change failing?"

**Inversion analysis:**
- "What would guarantee this transition fails?" -> Don't do those things
- "What are we NOT being told by the new supplier?" -> The silence is data
- "What capability of the old supplier are we taking for granted?" -> Test it

**Shadow sensing during transition:**
- Monitor old supplier behavior changes (they know before you tell them)
- Watch new supplier's other customers (are they also onboarding too many?)
- Track lead time stability (first 3 orders are always on time - month 6 reveals truth)

## Key Questions for Supply Chain Research

**Network:** What is the single point of failure? Where is Tier 2+ visibility zero? Which node's failure causes maximum cascade?

**Disruption:** What stopped happening in the last 90 days? What are competitors doing differently? What would an insurance underwriter see that we don't?

**Continuity:** What is the minimum viable network? How fast can we activate alternatives? What is the cost of 1 day / 1 week / 1 month disruption?

**Change:** Who benefits from this change failing? What assumption have we not tested? What does month 6 look like, not month 1?
